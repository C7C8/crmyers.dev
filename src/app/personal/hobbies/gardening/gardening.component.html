Gardening

Everyone needs a covid-19 hobby, right? Mine was gardening! I always kind of wanted to have a garden with useful plants in it (like herbs!), so when my girlfriend brought me a basil plant and a rosemary plant, my small scale gardening really took off!

Original Trio

My original garden had a rosemary plant, a basil plant, and a pot for thyme that I had just planted. I kept it by a window, but this wasn't nearly enough…

 More!

I could not resist adding more, and soon added pots for thyme, oregano, and dill.

 More, and purple!

I soon moved into a new apartment that had only east-facing windows, so I bought a small low-power grow light to keep my plants happy

 Going big

Turns out that blurple light wasn't very powerful, so I gave in and bought a very powerful one. And I added still more plants, including different basil varieties, sage, jalapenos, tobasco peppers, tomatoes, and onions.

Around this point, Amazon started recommending books on growing marijuana to me. Can't imagine why.

 Transplant

You can probably tell that these pots were much too small for the plants, so I upgraded. I wanted to get a giant planter and put them all together, but it turns out that buying a 50 gallon utility tote, punching holes in the bottom, and using the lid as the water basin works just as well and is actually far cheaper.

By the time I got around to doing this, my bigger plants were badly root bound. Unfortunately the rosemary didn't survive (soil didn't have enough drainage and I wasn't watering it right) so it isn't in these pictures. The jalapeno, tomato, and tobasco pepper plants were moved into a second identical tub outdoors, where the tomatoes grew absolutely enormous.

 Current: Dual planters

There wasn't enough vertical space for my plants to grow in the previous setup, so I moved them to a new spot in my apartment and positioned the light higher. After winter hit the pepper and tomato plants outside died (as I hoped; they were overcrowded) so I brought that tub in and broke up + refertilized the soil. I now have basil (the original that my girlfriend gave me!), dill, rosemary, sage (seedlings), oregano, thyme, a tiny cactus, and -- soon -- garlic!

Of those plants…

  • The rosemary will stay perpetually potted as it needs special watering treatment and a soil mix high in perlite (for drainage).
  • The tiny cactus (another gift from my girlfriend) will remain in a tiny pot for similar reasons
  • The oregano will be planted in the soil in the tub soon, once it's grown more
  • The thyme is only with me until spring when I can give it back to my parents (it's not mine).
